How to enter a Bedrud room from the web app: pre-join preview, connect, basic media controls, and access gates.

## Pre-join (welcome shell)

Before media connects you typically see:

- Display **name**
- **Microphone** and **camera** preview
- **Device selectors** for mic, camera, and speaker (when available)

Set these before joining so the first connection uses the right devices.

## Join and connect

1. Click **Join** (or equivalent).
2. You should see a short loading shell, then the **connected** meeting UI — not an infinite spinner.
3. Other participants appear as tiles or avatars once they have joined.

If connection fails, check browser permissions, network, and that the server’s LiveKit/WebRTC ports are reachable. Operators: see [WebRTC Connectivity](/en/docs/architecture/webrtc-connectivity).

## Basic controls

| Action | Expected behavior |
|--------|-------------------|
| Mute / unmute mic | Other participants stop/start hearing you |
| Camera on / off | Your video tile updates for others |
| Leave room | You disconnect cleanly; others should not keep a zombie participant forever |
| Rejoin | Joining the same room again works after leave |

## Multiple participants

Use two browsers (or normal + private) to confirm:

- Both sides see each other’s tiles
- Mute and camera state is visible remotely

## Gated rooms

Rooms may require a **password**, wait for approval, or other access checks depending on room settings. Complete the **room access dialog** when shown, then proceed to pre-join / join.

Guest join (no account) may be allowed on some instances — share a link and guests enter with a display name only. Guest join fails closed if the room is full or capacity tracking fails (no media token). Guests get a **stable signed identity cookie** (`bedrud_guest_id`) so room bans stick across revisits. Lobby **count** for public rooms may be public; participant identity lists require authentication.
