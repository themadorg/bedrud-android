Self-hosted video is not only “join and see faces.” Bedrud’s meeting shell now covers presentation layouts, richer chat, optional watch parties, and push-to-talk — still in one binary on your hardware.

This post is a map. Deep how-tos live under **Using Bedrud** in the docs.

## Stage mode

Present without fighting a free-for-all grid. Hosts can start **stage**, share the screen as a stage overlay, keep a participant strip, and end stage to return to normal layout. Late joiners should land in the correct stage state.

→ [Stage Mode](/en/docs/using/stage-mode)

## Collaborative whiteboard

Shared drawing with Yjs over LiveKit data channels. Multi-user strokes, select/move/delete, optional experimental toggle.

→ [Collaborative Whiteboard](/en/docs/using/whiteboard) · [Engineering post](/en/blog/collaborative-whiteboard)

## YouTube watch

Paste a URL, play roughly in sync, close for everyone. Also available behind Experimental settings when gated.

→ [YouTube Watch](/en/docs/using/youtube-watch)

## Chat: reactions, polls, attachments

In-room text is not a stub:

- Emoji **reactions** and composer **emoji picker**
- **Polls** with live vote updates
- **Images / files** within quota (chunked for large payloads)
- Client-side retention caps so long sessions stay bounded

→ [In-Meeting Chat](/en/docs/using/chat)

## Push-to-talk

Enable PTT in audio settings, capture a hold key, transmit only while held. Disable to restore normal mute toggle. Device switch mid-call without full reconnect.

→ [Audio & Push-to-Talk](/en/docs/using/audio-and-push-to-talk)

## Dashboard and account basics

Before the call: register, avatar, recent rooms, quick-join by slug or URL.

→ [Account & Settings](/en/docs/using/account-and-settings) · [Dashboard & Rooms](/en/docs/using/dashboard-and-rooms) · [Joining a Meeting](/en/docs/using/joining-a-meeting)

## Moderation and layout

Screen share outside stage, spotlight, room info, role-based context menus, and transport fallback UX when the network is hostile.

→ [Meeting Controls](/en/docs/using/meeting-controls) · [User Roles](/en/docs/guides/roles)

## What is still planned

**Recording** routes remain a planned/operator-facing path — do not treat full egress recording as universally production-complete on every release until docs un-gate it. Infrastructure, admin, LiveKit, and deploy guides stay the source of truth for operators.
